1.YOUNGBLOOD

Released in 2016

Youngblood starts with its title track; the simple strums and honest vocals of frontman Luke Hemmings. Its interest slowly creeps with light layers of percussion and synth. However, it’s once the hard-hitting bass in its chorus roars that you can fully fathom the direction of this album. The punk sensibilities of 5 Seconds Of Summer have been hung and left to dry on this record. In 5 Seconds Of Summer’s retrospect, change has been a good thing. They’ve reached their second Australian #1 with this title track alone and taken over radio airwaves around the world and all in all, Youngblood is already promising.

3. SOUNDS GOOD FEELS GOOD

Released in 2018

The Album of Sounds Good Feels Good came out in 2016. Teenagers swooned, tours sold out and millions of records were shifted. Their effervescent, ultra-melodic blueprint is refined here, with tracks like "Jet Black Heart," "Broken Home," and “Airplanes" showcasing a maturing subtlety. But no one unleashes boisterous anthems better. The opening salvo in particular–“Money," "She’s Kinda Hot," and "Hey Everybody!"–is the very appealing sound of a band having the time of their lives.
